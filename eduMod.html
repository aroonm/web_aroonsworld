St. Patricks' Boys

Over weekends I help kids who live in a shelter home by providing them after school support in Mathematics. More than that, I strive to serve as a role model for these kids by supporting, mentoring and encouraging them in positive ways.

One evening my student, Chetan said that he loves music and effortlessly began to play a progressively complex beat on our desk with his fingers. I was quite impressed because he never received any training, it was something that came naturally to him. Another kid, once showed me his sketchbook asking for my opinion because he knew I loved art, and honestly I was very impressed again because his artwork was very good considering he'd never studied art or attended art class.

My point is, every child has immense potential. It just gets subdued by the reality of their everyday lives. They fall victim to the viscious circle they've seen the people around them in which makes them feel hopeless and makes them blind to what they can achieve. But deep down, I've seen - They do have a desire to learn, they constantly question things around them and this is why I want to spend time with them and teach them. If we can actually utilize their enthusiasm effectively, I'm positive that all our children will one day attain an excellent education.

Dream Camp 2016

Every year, we as MAD (Make A Difference) take our kids out for a "Dream Camp" where we let our kids have fun but also make sure they learn some hopefully valuable and useful life lessons. For example, this DC we encouraged our kids to talk about their dreams and what drives them. We had appreciation boxes through which kids could express their appreciation to others.
